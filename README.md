## 7. The Topological Demand Mesh (TDM)

This is the most radical concept in NEEP.

**Current thinking:** Demand is a number. 1000 people want this product. Price it at $50.

**NEEP thinking:** Demand is a shape. It has holes, clusters, ridges, and phase transitions. That shape tells you things no number can.

### What the TDM Reveals That No Existing System Can:

**Demand Clusters (Betti-0):** Groups of buyers with nearly identical intent. If the mesh shows 3 tight clusters, you have 3 completely different buyer personas willing to pay 3 different prices — automatically detected, no A/B test required.

**Demand Loops (Betti-1):** Cyclical patterns where buyers return to the same product at predictable intervals. A loop means there is a recurrence pattern — the buyer will be back in exactly X days regardless of current price. This is valuable: you can price low now to convert, and the loop tells you when to price high.

**Demand Voids (Betti-2):** Price points that create a topological hole in the demand manifold — a price nobody will ever pay under current market conditions. Current systems only discover these voids AFTER failed sales. TDM reveals them before a single transaction.

**Phase Transitions:** The most powerful signal. When the topology of demand is about to undergo a sudden restructuring — like a phase transition in physics — the Betti numbers change in a predictable way before the actual demand shift. This gives NEEP a 10-30 second window of price prediction that no other system has.

| Pause distribution | Many short pauses | Few long pauses on key info |
| Tab switching | Comparing competitors | Committed to this product |
| Re-read frequency | Confused | Confirming details to buy |
| Zoom behavior | Casual inspection | Quality verification |

**Shannon Entropy formula applied to commerce:**

```
H(buyer) = -SUM[ p(action_i) * log2(p(action_i)) ]
```
